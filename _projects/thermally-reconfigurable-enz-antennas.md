### Overview
Using **phase-change materials** (VO₂, GST) and ENZ substrates (Si:In₂O₃, CdO) we build nanophotonic antennas whose resonance, Q-factor and radiation pattern can be toggled in situ. Transient Joule and optical heating drive micro-Kelvin-per-milliwatt efficiency, while ENZ-enhanced field confinement magnifies thermo-optic shifts by 10×.

### Key contributions
1. **VO₂ metasurface modulators** – demonstrated >90 % amplitude modulation at 2 µm using ultrafast pump-probe switching.
2. **ENZ-coupled dielectric antennas** – our *ACS Photonics* 2025 study achieved 5 nm/V tuning with Q > 150.
3. **Thermal meta-lenses** – binary-phase CdO pillars reconfigure focal length from 50–120 µm with 10 mW heating.

### Impact
Reconfigurable ENZ antennas pave the way for **adaptive infrared cameras, programmable wavefront shaping and dynamic thermal emitters** crucial for sensing and free-space communications.
